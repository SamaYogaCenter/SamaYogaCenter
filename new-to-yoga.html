What to Expect

👋

Arrive 10-15 Minutes Early

Give yourself time to check in, store your belongings, set up your mat, and settle in before class begins. Our staff will help you get oriented.

🧘‍♀️

No Experience Needed

Our teachers welcome absolute beginners. They'll offer modifications and provide guidance throughout class. Go at your own pace—yoga is not a competition.

🧘

Listen to Your Body

Honor where you are today. It's perfectly fine to take breaks, modify poses, or skip something that doesn't feel right. Your practice is your own.

✨

Stay for Savasana

The final resting pose is one of the most important parts of practice. This is where your body integrates the benefits of your practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be flexible to do yoga?

Absolutely not! Flexibility is something you develop through yoga practice—it's not a prerequisite. Our teachers provide modifications for every pose, making the practice accessible to all body types and flexibility levels.

Which class should I start with?

We recommend Hatha or Restorative Yoga for complete beginners. These classes move at a slower pace and focus on foundational poses. Once you're comfortable, you can explore Vinyasa Flow or other styles. Feel free to ask our staff for personalized recommendations!

What if I can't keep up with the class?

That's perfectly okay! Yoga is a personal practice, not a performance. Take breaks in Child's Pose whenever you need to. Our teachers will offer modifications, and no one is judging you—everyone is focused on their own practice.

How often should I practice?

Even once a week can provide benefits! Many students find that 2-3 times per week helps them progress steadily while allowing recovery time. Listen to your body and find a rhythm that works for your schedule and energy levels.

Is yoga religious or spiritual?

While yoga has spiritual roots, our classes are secular and welcoming to people of all beliefs (or no beliefs). You can approach yoga as purely physical exercise, or explore its deeper philosophical aspects—it's entirely up to you.
